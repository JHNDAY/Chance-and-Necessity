üéØ The Critical Difference: C=C Double Bond Geometry

The ONLY difference: The spatial arrangement around the C=C double bond. This creates two distinct geometric isomers:

  • Trans (Fumaric): COOH groups on OPPOSITE sides of the double bond
  • Cis (Maleic): COOH groups on the SAME side of the double bond

Unlike single bonds, the C=C double bond cannot rotate, locking these molecules into their specific geometric arrangements!

Fumaric Acid (trans)

  • Geometry: COOH groups opposite
  • Stability: More stable (lower energy)
  • Melting Point: 287¬∞C (higher)
  • Solubility: Less soluble in water
  • Occurrence: Found in nature
  • Uses: Food additive, Krebs cycle

Maleic Acid (cis)

  • Geometry: COOH groups same side
  • Stability: Less stable (higher energy)
  • Melting Point: 131¬∞C (lower)
  • Solubility: More soluble in water
  • Occurrence: Synthetic origin
  • Uses: Industrial polymers, resins

Steric Effects: In maleic acid, the COOH groups are forced close together, creating repulsion and strain.

Hydrogen Bonding: Maleic acid can form intramolecular hydrogen bonds, affecting its properties.

Symmetry: Fumaric acid's higher symmetry leads to better crystal packing and higher melting point.

Reactivity: The different geometries affect how these molecules interact with other compounds and enzymes.
